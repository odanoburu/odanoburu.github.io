On my one-year civil service exam taker career

In Brazil many jobs in the public sector are filled via civil service exams (‘concursos públicos’), as a way of selecting professionals with little risk of influence peddling or bias (which is not to say these exams are equitable, hence why there are also affirmative action measures). These exams also serve as a selection process for companies whose majority owner is the government, such as the national oil company Petrobras.

Brazilians generally view public sector jobs as desirable, given that they usually pay more than their private sector counterparts (for those jobs that do have private sector counterparts), and are more stable (some even have stability guarantees, but not those in companies such as Petrobras).
